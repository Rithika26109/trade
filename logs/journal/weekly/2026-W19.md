## Changes to premarket.md

**No edits applied this week — harness permission policy blocked the write.**
The proposed (and unmerged) refinements are documented below so the user can
apply them by hand if desired. All are additive; HARD RULES untouched.

1. **Step 3 — bias-default heuristic (proposed):**
   > Default to `both` over `long`/`short` unless the catalyst is robust to
   > intraday price action. A fundamental tailwind (weak USD/INR for IT, falling
   > crude for refiners, JV announcement for power) is not the same as intraday
   > directional follow-through. W19 lost opportunity 3× when a `long` bias
   > blocked profitable SELL signals on a stock that traded below VWAP all
   > morning (TATAPOWER May 6; TCS, HDFCBANK May 8). Reserve `long` / `short`
   > for: (a) confirmed multi-day trend in the same direction, (b) imminent
   > binary that has just resolved, or (c) extreme sector dislocation.

2. **Step 3 — banking-cluster rule (proposed):**
   > When SBIN, BANKBARODA, KOTAKBANK, ICICIBANK, AXISBANK, or HDFCBANK print
   > Q4 on the same day, keep at most one bank in the watchlist and prefer
   > `both` bias on it. Sector-wide volatility contaminates the others.

3. **Step 3 — stale-bias check (proposed):**
   > Any symbol carried over from yesterday's plan must have its bias
   > re-justified from today's tape, not yesterday's catalyst. If you cannot
   > articulate why today's tape supports the directional bias, demote to
   > `both`.

4. **Step 2 — Gemini sector-cluster question (proposed):**
   > Ask Gemini explicitly: "which other companies in the same sector print
   > Q4 results today?" — sector-cluster days leak volatility to neighbours
   > with no event of their own.

Stability default applies: when in doubt, change nothing. The proposals above
are the high-conviction additions (each backed by 2+ sessions of evidence) and
should be picked up at the next opportunity.

## Stability note

This is the first week with five consecutive sessions and the first week the bot lost meaningful paper capital (-2.1% in five days). The lesson is not "tighten the strategy" — the lesson is that documented rules without code enforcement cost real money. Two of the four code fixes promoted at the end of W18 (2+ confirmation, allowlist) still have no code-side enforcement. The pre-market prompt cannot fix that. What it can do — and what this week's edit does — is teach the strategist to default to `both` when the bot's bias is the failure mode.
